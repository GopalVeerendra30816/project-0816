Title description, May 2, 2016

More Hats!The prevailing type of power steering from the 1950s to the early 2000s was hydraulic assist. Hydraulic power steering uses, as its name indicates, hydraulic fluid that's pressurized by a pump driven off the engine.

Although it served the automotive world well for those 50 years, there are a few downsides to this type of system: there's wasted energy, since the pump is running continuously, even when the vehicle is driving straight and there's no assist needed.
